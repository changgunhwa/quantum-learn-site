Core Technology

We are developing a new type of microwave-based quantum radar, incorporating quantum entanglement, quantum spin control, and quantum dot technologies. Our system operates without the need for complex quantum receivers or optical-to-microwave conversion systems, enabling energy-efficient and real-time detection under all conditions.

Key Features

  • Direct quantum spin control with enhanced diffraction sensitivity
  • Microwave-based quantum sensing with 100% energy efficiency
  • Consistent detection performance across all distances
  • Real-time response with minimal power consumption

Principle

Quantum radar functions by transmitting one particle of an entangled pair (the signal) toward a potential target, while retaining the other particle (the idler) in a controlled quantum state.

The detection process leverages quantum phase interference between diffracted signal quantum and the preserved idler quantum to determine the presence or absence of a target.

Even in non-entangled environments where quantum entanglement is destroyed, quantum radar still maintains a superior Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR) compared to classical radar systems.

* Distance and velocity measurement functions are currently under theoretical development.

Technical Features

  • Robust Against Noise: Maintains high detection performance even in environments with strong thermal noise.
  • Stealth Detection Capability: Detects objects with very small Radar Cross Section (RCS).
  • Non-Invasive: Low-intensity signals minimize detectability, ideal for stealth operations.
  • Post-Entanglement Advantage: Effective even after the loss of quantum entanglement due to retained correlations.

Applications

  • Stealth Vehicle Detection: Identifies low-RCS aircraft, drones, submarines, and other covert targets.
  • Military & Defense Surveillance: Ideal for high-security reconnaissance with low probability of detection.
  • Low-Power Radar Systems: Future-ready sensors with minimal energy consumption and extended range.
  • Detection in Noisy Environments: Outperforms classical radar in scenarios with high environmental noise.
